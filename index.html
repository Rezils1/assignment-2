Biography

Inventor Of The Atomic Bomb Robert Oppenheimber J. Robert Oppenheimer (1904-1967) was an American theoretical physicist and one of the most influential scientists of the 20th century. He is often referred to as the "father of the atomic bomb" for his role in the Manhattan Project, the World War II research and development project that produced the first nuclear weapons.

Oppenheimer was born in New York City and displayed exceptional intelligence from a young age. He studied at Harvard University, where he excelled in languages, science, and philosophy. After completing his Ph.D. in theoretical physics at the University of GÃ¶ttingen in Germany, Oppenheimer returned to the United States and began teaching at the University of California, Berkeley.

During World War II, Oppenheimer was appointed as the scientific director of the Manhattan Project, leading a team of scientists and engineers in the development of the atomic bomb. Under his supervision, the first successful test of an atomic bomb took place on July 16, 1945, in the New Mexico desert.

After the war, Oppenheimer became an advocate for international control of nuclear power and spoke out against the proliferation of nuclear weapons. However, during the Cold War, he faced scrutiny from the U.S. government due to his past associations with communist organizations. In 1954, his security clearance was revoked, effectively ending his career as a government advisor.
